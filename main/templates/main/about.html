Welcome to MythMakers

MythMakers is an online storytelling community using the power of the internet to create new and exciting stories in a range of media forms

Humanity has always been a race of storytellers. In the past it was how we communicated important information, how we made sense of the world around us and how we built a sense of community, through shared common experiences.

Throughout the course of human history, we have been resourceful in finding new ways of telling those stories. From ancient cave paintings to Egyptian hieroglyphics all the way to graffiti and emojis, there's no limit to the new ways we can find to communicate thoughts, feelings and emotions with each other.

With the advent of the internet, a whole world of new opportunities opened up. Over time, we realised that the tools we needed to tell stories in new and innovative ways were right at our fingertips, easily accessible and instantly available. Not only could authors stretch their influence to reach new readers all over the world, but new media forms became available for writers and storytellers to weave their magic.

YouTube and other video-sharing sites have become a hotbed of hugely creative visual storytelling experiments. The innovations that make podcasting easy and accessible have given rise to a whole new wave of audio drama that builds worlds and paints vivid pictures with nothing but sound.

But even with these new formats of storytelling, there was still a key part of humanity's fascination with storytelling missing: the community.

Shared Stories

It was this community aspect that was the germ of an idea that blossomed into MythMakers.

Where our ancestors may have gathered around a fire to tell stories, nowadays we congregate on the internet. But many of the places people gather are focused on telling a specific type of story, rather than the act of telling a story itself.

But what about the people who don't have a specific type of story they want to tell, just that urge to tell stories in a range of formats and styles? Where did they have to go?

For the longest time, the answer was nowhere. But now they have MythMakers, a safe and supportive community where storytellers from all backgrounds can come and share their stories.

Our Values
